For Parents and Guardians

Harm experienced in childhood can have long lasting effects for children and young people, and each child reacts in a different way. They may experience a range of emotional, psychological and physical problems as a result of being this abuse.

Why does child abuse happen?

Harm or risk of harm to children and young people can happen when stress, tiredness, lack of skills, information and support combine to make the pressure of caring for children overwhelming. Some of the factors that can contribute to the likehood of harm include:

  • Isolation and lack of support - When there is no one to help with parenting such as extended family, friends, a partner or community.
  • Stress - Taking care of family member, job worries, financial problem can increase stress and overwhelm parents.
  • Unrealistic Expectations - A lack of understanding of a child or young person's developing stages and behaviour.
  • Lack of parenting skills - Not knowing how to help children and young poeple learn, grow and behave in a positive way.
  • Drug and alcohol problems - Drug and alcohol addiction or abuse may limit a parent's ability to fulfill their children needs.
  • Poor childhood experience - The parent(s) may be abused during their childhood and it's creating intergenerational patterns of abuse.

Effects of Harm

  • Low self esteem
  • Increased fear, guilt and self blame
  • Distrust of adults
  • Depression
  • Suicidal thoughts and self harming
  • Anxiety, stress, learning disorders
  • Aggressive behaviour
  • Drug and alcohol abuse

Statistics and Facts

  • NOT everyone who abuses children is a pedophile.
  • Child abuse is commited by a wide range of individuals with different motivations.
  • Only about one third of child sexual abuse incidents are identified, and even fewer are reported.
  • 20% of children abused before the age of 8.
  • 90% of child sexual abuse victims know their abuser.
  • Use of alcohol or drugs at an early age can be a sign of trauma such as child sexual abuse.
  • 1 in 14 children has been physically abused.
  • Over 6,800 children were identified as needing protection from physical abuse last year (2015).
  • Neglect makes up for 78% of all reported abuse cases but is incredibly under-reported and often unnoticed.
